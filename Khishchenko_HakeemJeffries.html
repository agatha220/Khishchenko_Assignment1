Hakeem Jeffries Runs for Reelection to Congress as Minority House Leader

By: Agatha Khishchenko

October 13, 2024

Background

Jeffries is 54 years old, born in Brooklyn, NY. He grew up in Crown Heights, NY and graduated from SUNY Binghamton with a degree in political science. Jeffries went on to get a masters in public policy from Georgetown University and a law degree from NYU. He practiced law for almost a decade before running and winning a seat in the New York State Assembly in 2006. Jeffries served in the State Assembly until he ran for Congress in 2012.


Political History

Jeffries was elected to represent New Yorkâ€™s 8th Congressional District, an area that encompasses parts of Brooklyn and Queens, and has served in the House of Representatives since 2013. He is the former Chair of the Democratic Caucus, Whip of the Congressional Black Caucus, and co-chaired the Democratic Policy and Communications Committee. Jeffries is currently serving his sixth term in congress. When Nancy Pelosi, the former minority leader of the House, announced she would step down from the role, Jeffries ran unopposed and became the House Democratic minority leader on January 3, 2023.


Reelection

Jeffries is running for reelection in his current congressional district where he has served for over a decade. He is running against Republican John Delaney. Both Delaney and Jeffries had uncontested primaries.


Jeffries is a vocal opponent of MAGA and Project 2025

Jeffries has stood against MAGA Republicans and former President Trump. He has indicated that Democrats will oppose federal funding that pushes MAGA policies as part of Project 2025's agenda.

In an interview last month on CNN, Jeffries said that MAGA Republicans want to exercise control over the American people by trying to "jam Project 2025 down the throats of the American people."

Primary Policy Priorities

ProPublica analyzed Jeffries press releases and found that the top three policy areas he focused on were crime and law enforcement (18.2%), civil rights, liberties and minority issues (14.7%), and government operations and politics (10.2%).

Jeffries sponsored H.R. 5065, a bill that provides entrepreneuship training to formally incarcerated individauls. Although the bill passed in the House on January 8, 2020, it was never passed by the Senate and has not been approved into law.

Jeffries also sponsored H.R. 2093, a bill that made substantive language changes in the Department of Veterans Affairs. H.R. 2093 was singed into law by President Biden on November 22, 2021.


Potential Removal of Republicans from Committee Roles

Jeffries has taken a strong leadership role as the House Minority Leader. Recently, he has indicated that if the Democrates win the House, he will consider removing Republicans like Scott Perry and Ronny Jackson from the House Intelligence Committee. Speaker Mike Johnson had appointed both Perry and Jackson despite bipartisan disapproval.


Communication as House Minority Leader

Jeffries is one of the best orators in Congress, although he has a tendency to lean on his talking points and evading direct questions. In a recent interview with Vanity Fair when asked if Democrats would win back the House this fall, he gave a political answer about American people and freedom, and failed to answer the question directly.

That being said, his communication skils are one of the best and "have not gone unnoticed among his Democratic colleagues." Memphis Democrat Steve Cohen said "I don't think he knows what a teleprompter is."
